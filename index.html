MILEAGE The Aviator Was Right FACT CHECK // GREAT CIRCLE ROUTES
The Claim
"Planes have to fly north before heading to London or Ireland because of the curvature of the Earth."
- Uncle Bruce, The Aviator
Verdict: Confirmed

Bruce is right.

Flights from the eastern US to the UK/Ireland follow a great circle route that curves northward over Canada and the North Atlantic. It looks wrong on a flat map, but it's the shortest path on a sphere.

Why It Works
1
Earth is a sphere. The shortest path between two points is a great circle — a circle whose center is the center of Earth.
2
NYC (40.7N) and London (51.5N) are both in mid-latitudes. The great circle between them arcs up to ~53-55N latitude.
3
Flat maps (Mercator) distort this. A "straight line" on Mercator is actually longer on a sphere — it's called a rhumb line.
4
Airlines save ~200-400 miles by following the great circle. That's 30-60 minutes of flight time and thousands in fuel.
